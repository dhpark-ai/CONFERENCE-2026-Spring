Cobot Path Optimization Considering Obstacles, Vision and Joint Velocity Constraints

Donghyeok Park*, Changyeon Kim, Jeongun Lee, Hunok Choi
Korea Aerospace Industries (KAI), Robotics Team
*Corresponding author
📄 Download PDF

Abstract

This paper addresses the problem of task sequencing and path optimization for collaborative robots in aerospace manufacturing processes. Unlike conventional approaches that minimize travel distance in task space, the proposed method considers joint velocity constraints, obstacle avoidance, and vision-induced motion requirements. A unified cost function integrating joint-space travel time, back-off motion, vision acquisition, and task execution is formulated. The optimal task sequence is obtained by minimizing the total cycle time. Experimental results on a drilling task with 100 target points demonstrate that the proposed method achieves up to 3.1% reduction in total cycle time compared to conventional approaches. The results highlight the importance of joint-space-aware planning in real-world robotic applications.

Key Contributions

  • Unified cost model incorporating joint velocity, vision constraints, and execution time
  • Demonstration that task-space distance minimization is insufficient for real robots
  • Validation in realistic aerospace drilling scenario with measurable cycle time reduction
